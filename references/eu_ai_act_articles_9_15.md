# EU AI Act — High-Risk AI System Requirements (Articles 9-15)

Reference text for Regula compliance guidance. Based on Regulation (EU) 2024/1689.

---

## Article 9 — Risk Management System

High-risk AI systems shall have a risk management system established, implemented, documented, and maintained.

**Requirements:**
1. Identification and analysis of known and reasonably foreseeable risks
2. Estimation and evaluation of risks during intended use and reasonably foreseeable misuse
3. Evaluation of risks based on post-market monitoring data
4. Adoption of appropriate and targeted risk management measures

**Key Principles:**
- Continuous iterative process throughout the AI system's lifecycle
- Regular systematic updating
- Residual risks must be judged acceptable
- Testing procedures must be suitable

---

## Article 10 — Data and Data Governance

Training, validation, and testing data sets shall be subject to appropriate data governance practices.

**Requirements:**
1. Design choices for data sets documented
2. Data collection processes and origin documented
3. Relevant preparation processing operations (annotation, labelling, cleaning, enrichment)
4. Assessment of availability, quantity, and suitability of data sets
5. Examination of possible biases likely to affect health, safety, or fundamental rights
6. Identification of relevant data gaps or shortcomings and how they are addressed

**Key Principles:**
- Training data must be relevant, sufficiently representative, and free of errors to the best extent possible
- Training data must have appropriate statistical properties for the intended purpose
- Personal data processing must comply with GDPR

---

## Article 11 — Technical Documentation

Technical documentation shall be drawn up before the high-risk AI system is placed on the market or put into service.

**Requirements:**
- Follow Annex IV format
- Documentation must demonstrate compliance with Articles 9-15
- Must be kept up to date
- Must be made available to national competent authorities upon request

**Annex IV Contents:**
1. General description of the AI system
2. Detailed description of elements and development process
3. Monitoring, functioning, and control of the AI system
4. Risk management system description
5. Changes throughout the lifecycle
6. Standards applied
7. EU declaration of conformity

---

## Article 12 — Record-Keeping

High-risk AI systems shall technically allow for automatic recording of events (logs).

**Requirements:**
1. Recording of period of use (start/end date)
2. Reference database against which input data has been checked
3. Input data for which the search has led to a match
4. Identification of natural persons involved in verification of results

**Key Principles:**
- Logging capabilities must be appropriate to the intended purpose
- Must enable monitoring of operation and post-market monitoring
- Must ensure traceability of functioning throughout lifecycle

---

## Article 13 — Transparency and Provision of Information to Deployers

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed to ensure their operation is sufficiently transparent.

**Requirements:**
1. Instructions for use provided to deployers including:
   - Identity and contact details of the provider
   - Characteristics, capabilities, and limitations of performance
   - Intended purpose and foreseeable misuse scenarios
   - Changes made throughout the lifecycle
   - Human oversight measures
   - Expected lifetime and maintenance measures
   - Computational and hardware resources needed

**Key Principles:**
- Information must be concise, complete, correct, and clear
- Must be relevant, accessible, and comprehensible to deployers

---

## Article 14 — Human Oversight

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed to allow effective oversight by natural persons.

**Requirements:**
1. Oversight measures must enable individuals to:
   - Fully understand capacities and limitations of the system
   - Duly monitor the system's operation
   - Remain aware of automation bias
   - Correctly interpret the system's output
   - Decide not to use the system or disregard, override, or reverse the output
   - Intervene in the operation or interrupt the system

**Key Principles:**
- Proportionate to risks, level of autonomy, and context of use
- Must include ability to stop the system via a "stop" button or similar procedure

---

## Article 15 — Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity

High-risk AI systems shall be designed and developed to achieve appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity.

**Requirements:**
1. **Accuracy:** Appropriate levels declared in instructions for use
2. **Robustness:** Resilient to errors, faults, or inconsistencies; technical redundancy solutions
3. **Cybersecurity:** Resilient against unauthorised attempts to alter use or performance; protection against model poisoning, adversarial examples, confidentiality attacks

---

## Compliance Timeline

- **2 August 2026:** Full application of Articles 9-15 for high-risk AI systems
- Providers must ensure compliance before placing systems on the market
