How it works

01

Detect

Scans Python, JavaScript, TypeScript, Java, Go, Rust, C, and C++ for AI library imports, model files, and API call patterns.

02

Classify

Maps findings to EU AI Act risk tiers — prohibited, high-risk, limited-risk, minimal — with confidence scoring.

03

Report

Outputs SARIF for CI/CD pipelines, JSON for automation, HTML reports for DPOs and auditors.

What Regula is

Regula is

✓A development-time static analysis tool that detects AI code patterns and maps them to EU AI Act obligations
✓A shift-left compliance scanner — like ESLint for regulatory risk, running in your terminal or CI/CD
✓A starting point for compliance awareness, not a finish line

Regula is not

—A runtime monitoring system (it analyses source code, not running systems)
—A legal compliance certificate (findings are indicators, not legal determinations)
—A replacement for enterprise GRC platforms (it complements them)
—Legal advice (consult qualified counsel for compliance decisions)

Built for the August 2026 deadline

Prohibited AI practices have been banned since February 2025. High-risk obligations take effect August 2026. Regula gives your team visibility into which categories your code falls under — before enforcement, not after.

Your rules, your compliance

Define custom detection patterns in regula-rules.yaml. Extend the built-in rules with your organisation's specific requirements.

rules:
  prohibited:
    - name: internal_scoring
      patterns:
        - "employee.*scoring.*system"
      description: "Internal employee social scoring"
      article: "5"
