## Methodology

Each repository was scanned with `regula gap --project .` which runs the compliance
gap assessment for Articles 9–15. A score ≥ 50/100 is counted as "passing" for that article.
Files were scanned for Article 5 (prohibited) patterns using `regula check`.

Corpus: 5 Regula test fixture repositories representing the range of AI project types.

## Key Findings

- **0% of projects** demonstrate Article 9 (Risk Management) infrastructure — consistent with AIR Blackbox's 97% failure rate finding
- **Article 14 (Human Oversight)** scores highest at 20% pass rate, driven by `sample_compliant`'s explicit approval workflow
- **Article 12 (Record-Keeping)** is the only article where `sample_compliant` passes (65/100), reflecting Regula's own audit trail implementation
- **Article 10 (Data Governance)** scores 0 across all fixtures — typical of code-only repositories without data governance documentation
- The test corpus is small (5 fixtures); these numbers reflect fixture design, not the broader ecosystem

## Comparison with Published Benchmarks

AIR Blackbox (Feb 2026) scanned 5,754 files across Python AI repos and found:
- 97% fail Article 9 (Risk Management)
- 89% fail Article 12 (Record-Keeping)
- 84% fail Article 14 (Human Oversight)
- Average compliance score: 2.2/6 articles

Regula's benchmark assessed 5 fixture projects across 7 articles including Article 5 (prohibited)
and found: 0/5 pass Article 9, 1/5 pass Article 12, 1/5 pass Article 14 — directionally consistent
with AIR Blackbox's findings on the same articles.

The key difference: Regula also checks for Article 5 prohibited practices and the
exemptions framework under Article 6 — gaps that AIR Blackbox's 6-article scope misses.
Regula also covers Article 10 (data governance) and Article 15 (accuracy/security).
