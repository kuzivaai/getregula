The EU AI Act Omnibus Delay: What Developers Actually Need to Know

The Digital Omnibus on AI proposes to push the high-risk system deadline from August 2026 to late 2027 or mid-2028. The trilogue starts on 28 April. Here is what that means for your codebase, and what you should do about it today.

What's happening

On 19 November 2025, the European Commission published the Digital Omnibus on AI: a targeted simplification package proposing amendments to Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 (the EU AI Act). The headline change is a deferral of the application dates for high-risk AI system obligations.

Since then, two things have happened:

  • 13 March 2026 — the Council of the EU adopted its general approach, broadly endorsing the Commission's deferral proposal with targeted amendments on Article 6(3) scope and regulatory sandbox timelines.
  • 26 March 2026 — the European Parliament voted 569–45–23 (for–against–abstentions) in favour of its first-reading position, supporting the deferral but introducing amendments to strengthen Article 50 transparency requirements and to narrow the Article 6(3) procedural-task carve-out. Trilogue negotiations launched the same day.

Both institutions now have their negotiating mandates. Trilogue negotiations are targeted for 28 April 2026. This is when the Council, Parliament, and Commission sit down to hammer out a compromise text. Until the trilogue produces an agreed text that is formally adopted, the Omnibus is not law.

Should you wait or act now?

The honest answer: it depends on your risk tier.

If your system is prohibited (Article 5)

Nothing has changed. The prohibitions on social scoring, subliminal manipulation, real-time remote biometric identification in public spaces, and emotion inference in workplaces and education have been in force since 2 February 2025. The Omnibus does not touch these. If Regula flags your code as prohibited-tier, act now.

If your system is high-risk (Annex III — standalone)

The Omnibus proposes to move your compliance deadline from 2 August 2026 to 2 December 2027. Both Council and Parliament have endorsed this deferral in principle. It is very likely (though not certain) that the trilogue will agree to some version of this delay.

That said, "very likely" is not "law". The Commission already missed its own 2 February deadline for publishing Article 6 guidance on high-risk classification. Regulatory timelines in this space have a track record of slipping in both directions. Planning purely on the assumption of a delay is a gamble.

The practical advice: keep your compliance programme running, but deprioritise the sprint to August 2026. You almost certainly have more time than the current law says, but you do not have an excuse to do nothing.

If your system is high-risk (Annex I — product-embedded)

The proposed deadline is 2 August 2028. If you are building AI into medical devices, machinery, or other products covered by the Union harmonisation legislation listed in Annex I, you have more runway — but you also face the additional burden of aligning with sector-specific conformity assessment procedures that are still being drafted by CEN-CENELEC JTC 21. The harmonised standards (including prEN 18286 on AI quality management systems) are not expected before Q4 2026 at the earliest.

If your system is minimal or limited risk

The Omnibus does not affect you materially. Limited-risk transparency obligations under Article 50 are already in effect or unaffected by the proposed deferrals. If anything, Parliament's position strengthens Article 50 requirements, so the trilogue could result in tighter transparency rules for chatbots, deepfakes, and emotion recognition.

What Regula does about this

Regula tracks every regulatory change in a structured delta log: a versioned, primary-sourced changelog for the EU AI Act. Each entry records the date, affected articles, source URL, and impact on Regula's detection patterns.

The delta log currently contains entries for:

  • Regulation adoption (12 July 2024)
  • Commission Omnibus proposal (19 November 2025)
  • Council general approach (13 March 2026)
  • Parliament first-reading position, 569–45–23 (26 March 2026)
  • Trilogue target date (28 April 2026)

The regula timeline command shows you which deadlines apply to your codebase based on your current risk classification. When the trilogue concludes, the delta log will be updated with the compromise text, and the timeline output will reflect the new dates.

The regula regwatch command warns you when your installed ruleset is older than the latest delta-log entry, so you know when to update.

What to do this week

  1. Find out your risk tier

    Run regula check . in your project root. This scans your codebase against 404 risk patterns and tells you which EU AI Act tier you are in — prohibited, high-risk, limited, or minimal. If you are not high-risk, the Omnibus delay does not change your obligations.

  2. Check whether the delay affects you

    If regula check classifies you as high-risk, run regula timeline to see your current deadline. Then check whether your system falls under Annex III (standalone, proposed delay to December 2027) or Annex I (product-embedded, proposed delay to August 2028). This determines how much additional runway you may get.

  3. Follow the delta log

    The delta log is updated as regulatory changes are confirmed. Once the trilogue produces an outcome (expected late April or May 2026), the log will reflect the agreed text. Keep your Regula installation current with pip install --upgrade regula-ai.

$ pipx install regula-ai && regula timeline copy
